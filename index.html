How to protect yourself and people around you

Wash your hands frequently

Regularly and thoroughly clean your hands with an alcohol-based hand rub or wash them with soap and water.

Maintain social distancing

Maintain at least 1 metre (3 feet) distance between yourself and anyone who is coughing or sneezing.

Don't touch your face

Hands touch many surfaces and can pick up viruses. From there, the virus can enter your body and can make you sick.

If you feel sick, seek medicine care early

Stay home if you feel unwell. Seek medical attention and call. Follow the directions of your local health authority.

Weark mask

You could spread virus to others even if you do not feel sick. Wear a wear a mask when you have to go out in public.

Cover coughs and sneezes

If you are in a private setting and do not have on your face mask, remember to always cover your mouth and nose.

Symptons

Common symptons:
  • tiredness
  • fever
  • dry cough
Some people may experience:
  • aches and pain
  • nasal congestion
  • runny nose
  • sore throat
